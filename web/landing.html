The three lifts

One judge, three rulebooks

Each lift is judged on its own checkpoint and its own referee command sequence — the same signal lamp and progress bar, tuned to the rules of the movement.

Squat

The hip crease must break below the top of the knee, with no downward movement on the ascent, framed by the referee's commands.

Two ways to train

Training and competition

Training Mode

Free reps, every rep called

Pick a weight, start a set, and get a live GOOD or NO LIFT call on every rep with the specific fault flagged. Sets are logged to your browser history — exportable to JSON.

Competition Mode

The computer plays referee

Set up still and locked out. The system issues the real referee commands for your lift, judges the single attempt on the full rulebook, and reveals the verdict as three lights.

Under the hood

How a rep gets judged

01

Pose

Webcam frames stream to a local YOLO pose model that tracks your joints in real time.

02

Checkpoints

Depth, chest touch, and lockout are measured frame-by-frame against the rulebook for your lift.

03

Commands

In competition, a referee state machine times the commands and watches for early movement.

04

Verdict

Each rep gets one call — with the specific faults named, not just a pass or fail.

The calls

Honest verdicts

Good

The lift met every checkpoint and obeyed every command. Three white lights.

No Lift

A rule was broken — the specific fault is named: insufficient depth, no chest touch, soft lockout.

Uncertain

Genuinely too close to call from this camera angle. White Lights says so instead of guessing.

Real judging runs locally. Start the backend with uvicorn api.main:app and open /live — the pose model needs your machine. Without a backend, the live page falls back to a clearly-labelled simulator so you can explore the interface.
